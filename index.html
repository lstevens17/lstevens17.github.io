About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Wellcome Sanger Institute, working on genomic analysis and computational methods for understanding biological variation and evolution. My research focuses on large-scale sequence datasets, comparative genomics, and reproducible computational workflows.

Comparative genomics

I develop and apply comparative genomic approaches to understand evolutionary processes across diverse taxa, with an emphasis on high-quality genome assemblies and large-scale datasets.

Computational methods

My work involves building scalable, reproducible computational pipelines for analysing genomic data, integrating modern bioinformatics tools with robust software engineering practices.

Evolutionary biology

I am interested in how genomic variation underpins adaptation and evolutionary change, particularly in non-model organisms and complex biological systems.
